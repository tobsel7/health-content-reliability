Autism typically not diagnosed until age 2 or 3

Posted: 06/07/2010 10:42:38 PM MDT

Early warning signs of autism may be present at 12 months, but a formal diagnosis typically isn't made until age 2 or 3, according to experts.

That's because the main signs of autism, such as delayed speech and fixations on objects, require language and motor skills that babies don't yet have.

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that doctors begin screening babies for autism at 18 months.

The average age when autistic children show skill regression is 18 to 21 months, according to Sally Ozonoff, a professor at the University of California at Davis who researches young children with autism.

Her research, and that of others, has found that babies from 9 months to 1 year may show subtle early signs of autism -- but at 6 months, "typical" babies and those later diagnosed as autistic don't show statistical differences in areas such as language development, social smiles and eye contact.

According to FirstSigns.org, warning signs at 12 months include lack of eye contact, lack of joyful expressions, lack of response to name, lack of babbling and lack of using gestures to communicate.

Babies who exhibit several of those signs should be considered at risk, experts say, but may not be given an autism diagnosis later in life.
