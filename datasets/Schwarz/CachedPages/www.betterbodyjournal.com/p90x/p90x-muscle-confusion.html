P90X Muscle Confusion

You're reading Part 2 of 11 in our
P90X series.

So you want to know about Muscle Confusion and how it fits into the P90X system?

What Happens When You Exercise

This is what happens when you begin a new workout program: If you haven’t been to the gym in a long time, or are just starting your first ever exercise program, then you’ll notice the day after your workout, you are really sore. It may take you a few days to recover from this soreness. This is because your muscles have been torn apart (on a microscopic level), and are rebuilding. As the muscles rebuild, they get stronger and bigger.

The more you workout during your exercise program, the less time it will take you to recover from muscle soreness. Eventually, you will very rarely get sore from working out, assuming you are doing the same routine over and over.

Why is Muscle Confusion important?

If you stick to an exercise routine, you start looking better and feeling great. But after a few months of the same routine, you will stop seeing the same, great results. Eventually, the returns you get from exercise start to diminish to almost nothing. (As you’ll tell from a lack of any soreness in your muscles)

The diminishing return of doing the same exercises over and over is called a “plateau.” It is common to bodybuilders and athletes, and even common for regular people. If you are already in good shape, you start to notice the “plateau” effect faster than someone who does not have good conditioning.

This is where muscle confusion comes in. You have to change up your exercise routine every so often, otherwise your muscles stop growing. If you do the same exercises with the same weight or resistance, month after month, there will come a point where you are just wasting your time.

So you either have to change up the routine you are doing, or increase the intensity. For maximum results, you want to do both.

Our bodies are incredibly efficient machines. We become accustomed to doing the same exercise fairly quickly, even if you are not in peak physical shape. We can see results from doing a new exercise immediately, but if you only stick to that particular exercise, the results diminish over a few weeks.

P90X Maximizes Results with Muscle Confusion

Another name for muscle confusion is cross-training, and the concept is nothing new. In fact, it has been around for quite some time. It was pioneered decades ago by the famous bodybuilder Joe Weider.

Over time, strength and sports coaches have used the idea of muscle confusion, or cross-training, to get the most out of their athletes.

Now the practice of muscle confusion is more mainstream, and systems like P90X have made it easy for regular people to understand and implement.

Different Exercises

One way to cause muscle confusion is by introducing new exercises into your workout regime. P90X has a full 12 DVDs of different exercises for each part of the body.

As you progress through the P90X program, you are introduced to different exercises for each body part. For example, you’ll learn different exercises that focus on the triceps, such as triceps extensions and dips.

By learning new exercises over time, your body never becomes accustomed to any exercise. Each new exercise works the muscles in a slightly different way, increasing the effectiveness of your workouts. With the variety you get, you never reach the “plateau” common to so many people who do not have the knowledge you do after going through P90X.

The main principle behind P90X is doing one month of the same routine, then doing a different variation of these exercises the next month. 30 days is the average amount of time that our bodies become accustomed to an exercise (assuming you do not increase intensity and are exercising fairly regularly)

Increased Intensity

In addition to new exercises, P90X takes it a step further by offering increasing levels of intensity. People new to proper exercise can progress at their own pace, while athletes and weightlifters can challenge themselves with some serious intensity.

You need to increase the difficulty of the exercises you do as your muscles grow. At some point in time, your muscles can easily handle the exercise and weight you are doing if nothing else changes. This is a recipe for getting bored of working out and giving up.

P90X takes the 30 day approach here, by increasing intensity as you complete each 30 day portion of the program.

By combining different exercises while increasing intensity, you can see why the P90X system has been so successful for so many people. P90X has been refined so that anyone can use it. Regular people can use P90X system to get into great physical shape, and athletes can use P90X reach peak physical conditioning.
