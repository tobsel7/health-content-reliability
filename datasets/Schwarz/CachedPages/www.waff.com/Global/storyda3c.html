Many worry H1N1 vaccine may have side effects

Posted: Updated:

By Elizabeth Gentle - bio | email
Posted by Dana Franks - email

HUNTSVILLE, AL (WAFF) - As state and local health leaders, along with schools, wait for the H1N1 vaccine to arrive, many are already worried the shot could have side effects.

Doctors aren't completely sure what the ingredients are that make up the H1N1 medication, or if it could cause an outbreak of reactions.

Since the first piece of information was released about swine flu, many have worried they will be next to catch it. They are also concerned the H1N1 vaccine won't be available to safeguard them.

"I think we are going to have a pandemic. I don't think there will be enough to go around, and they aren't going to let old people have it, so that sums up my concerns right there," said Huntsville resident Bob Adams.

The CDC recommends the H1N1 shot to those at high risk, like children 6 months and older, pregnant women, and health care workers.

But what side effects could there be from the vaccine?  Dr. Ali Hassoun said its too early to know.

"We really don't have enough information to know if there's anything extra from the local side effect," said Hassoun, Huntsville Hospital's Infectious Disease Specialist.

The last time the country had to produce the swine flu vaccine was in the 1970's. There were complications then with temporary muscle weakness and paralysis.

However, Hassoun said times have changed and so has technology.

"Where there's crowds, there's going to be more and more infections. The only way is through prevention," Hassoun said.

Hassoun said the best way to protect yourself is to get the shot.

Currently, the Centers for Disease Control is working with vaccine manufacturers to make sure that the H1N1 shot is safe, and that the flu has not changed in ways that would make the new vaccine ineffective.
