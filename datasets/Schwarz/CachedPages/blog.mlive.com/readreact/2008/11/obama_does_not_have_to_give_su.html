Obama's birthplace controversy won't go away

Published: Monday, November 24, 2008, 5:13 PM     Updated: Saturday, December 06, 2008, 1:42 PM
Joyce Pines | Kalamazoo Gazette
Follow
Share this story
Story tools
Charles Dharapak / APPresident-elect Barack Obama listens to a reporter's question during a news conference in Chicago on Nov. 24.

A reader called the Kalamazoo Gazette newsroom Monday afternoon and wanted to know why we hadn't run a story about the Supreme Court demanding that President-elect Barack Obama produce his birth certificate by Dec. 1.

Philadelphia Attorney Philip J. Berg has gone all the way to the highest court in the land in his effort to prove that Obama is not a United States citizen. Berg and his fellow believers claim that Obama was born in Kenya and thus is not eligible to be president of the United States.

More than 55,000 people have signed a petition online demanding that Obama provide documentation certifying that he is eligible to hold the office of president.

Asked to explain where he had gotten the information about Obama being forced to give up his birth certificate, the caller said he'd found it on the U.S. Supreme Court's docket. If you click on the link, you'll notice it shows that a petition was filed. The caller assumed this meant that Obama has to produce his certificate.

It does not mean that. It only means a petition was filed. The Supreme Court only agrees to hear a fraction of the cases that people bring to it each year. According to WorldNetDaily, the Web site which has followed this issue closely and has promoted speculation about Obama's birthplace, the justices have agreed to include this case among others that they will discuss in a private conference on Dec. 5. The other cases also include one challenging John McCain's citizenship.

The Gazette's caller explained he was a former police officer. He said when he was on the job and someone didn't want to do something he asked them to do -- he gave the example of asking a driver in a traffic stop to open his car's glove box -- then that was a good sign the individual was hiding something. So why, he asked, is Obama hiding his birth certificate?

The answer is that he hasn't hidden it. Obama's birth certificate can be viewed online at FactCheck.org, which provides close-up photos of the raised seal and the official state signature. When I explained this to the caller, he said he'd heard the copies online were fakes. I asked him if he'd actually gone to FactCheck. No, he said, he hadn't. He had his laptop with him and called up the site as we were talking. He said he'd check it out and hung up.

Going back to the caller's original query about why the mainstream media hasn't been writing about this controversy, the answer is that the mainstream media believes the question has been answered. FactCheck, Politifact and Snopes have all verified that Obama's Hawaiian birth certificate is legitimate. On Nov. 1, the Honolulu Advertiser also reported that Hawaii's state health director "personally inspected Obama's birth certificate and found it to be valid."

Further, as it is a requirement of the office of president that the person running be a "natural born citizen" of the United States, documentation of citizenship would be required in advance in order to file the necessary paperwork to get on the ballots of all 50 states. I had to produce my passport to prove I'm a citizen so I can teach a couple of journalism classes next semester at the local university. I imagine running for the job of president involves producing a lot more documentation.

Obama's detractors have gone so far as to move to Hawaii to gain residency status to try and obtain a copy of his birth certificate, but Hawaii's privacy laws are strict and only allow members of an individual's family access. A lawsuit has been filed in California to prevent that state's electors from casting their ballots for Obama until the issue of his birthplace is cleared up. There appear to be no end to efforts to keep questioning the validity of his birth in the United States.

In a story in the online magazine Salon: "According to several experts in conspiracy theories, and in the psychology of people who believe in conspiracy theories, there's little chance those people who think Obama is barred from the presidency will ever be convinced otherwise."

For those intent on believing that Obama is not an American citizen, would his giving the Supreme Court a copy of his birth certificate end the controversy? My guess is someone will start another rumor suggesting that some deal was made because it is too late to change the outcome of the election.
