Thu Jun 17, 2:18 pm ET

Tea party activists angered by Chris Matthews' recent documentary, "Rise of the New Right," are now threatening to boycott sponsors of his daily MSNBC show, "Hardball."

Conservative groups joining the anti-Matthews initiative include FreedomWorks, Kitchen Table Patriots, and the National Tea Party Foundation, according to the Washington Post.

Dick Armey, the former Republican Congressman and chairman of FreedomWorks, said Matthews' documentary was out of line.

"He's a better person than that," Armey said. "What's he doing? He's given himself over to something. I like him, personally, but it was sort of like — I had the feeling like my favorite nephew was doing something out of character. Chris, you're better than this."

Armey's group is pushing for a boycott of "Hardball" sponsor Dawn and its parent company, Proctor and Gamble.

Kitchen Table Patriots' Anna Puig, who is interviewed in the program, called the documentary a "propaganda piece" that "only serves a left-wing agenda." She also issued a statement calling for a boycott of Dawn.

While critics can blast Matthews for what they consider unfair coverage of their views, the documentary isn't simply footage of the host editorializing. It also includes a number of first-hand interviews and news clips showing tea party supporters and militia members voicing provocative claims about encroaching tyranny or supposed government oppression. Still, activists say that Matthews' selective editing of such clips portrayed the movement in the worst possible light.  

MSNBC declined to comment on the boycott threats.

If tea party activists go through with their plan, it won't be the first boycott of a 5 p.m. cable news host.

Activist groups, including ColorOfChange, have successfully steered advertisers away from running ads during Glenn Beck's Fox News show. That said, nearly a year after calls for a boycott gained steam, Beck remains one of the top-rated hosts on cable, handily beating Matthews in their shared time slot. 

 — Michael Calderone is the media writer for Yahoo! News.
