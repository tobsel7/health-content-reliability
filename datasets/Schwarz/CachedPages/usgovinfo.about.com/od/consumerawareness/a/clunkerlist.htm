Cash for Clunkers Car List?

Determining Your Trade-in’s Cash for Clunkers Eligibility

By Robert Longley, About.com Guide

See More About:

  • cash for clunkers
  • economic stimulus package
A cash for clunkers car list - a definitive list of vehicles eligible for the cash discounts offered by the Car Allowance Rebate System (CARS), a.k.a. "Cash for Clunkers" program -- is something everybody seems to be looking for. Unfortunately, the federal government chose not to produce such a list. Fortunately, there are a couple of resources you can use to "do-it-yourself."

Under the Cash for Clunkers program, consumers can receive a $3,500 or $4,500 discount for their eligible trade-in vehicle when purchasing or leasing a new, more fuel efficient vehicle.

Generally, under the Cash for Clunkers rules, any vehicle that has a "new" combined city/highway fuel economy of 18 miles per gallon or less (some very large pick-up trucks and cargo vans have different requirements), is no more than 25 years old and is drivable at the time of trade-in may qualify for a rebate. As a result there are literally thousands of vehicles that qualify.

Cash for Clunkers Eligibility Tools You Can Use
To help their dealers determine the Cash for Clunkers eligibility of both trade-in and new vehicles, the National Auto Dealers Association (NADA) provided its members with this Cash for Clunkers Eligibility Chart.

To help consumers and dealers determine Cash for Clunkers eligibility, the government has provided this online CARS Vehicle Eligibility Guide.

To use the CARS online eligibility tool, you will need to be able to provide some pretty specific information about the vehicle you want to trade in. Some things you may need to know include, the trade-ins model year, number of cylinders in the engine, the engine size (in liters), and the transmission type. You may also need to know your fuel type (gasoline or diesel), drive type (2-wheel or 4-wheel drive), Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR), or even whether or not your vehicle has a supercharger or turbocharger.

For more information and assistance with the Cash for Clunkers program, see the official Car Allowance Rebate System Web site - CARS.gov - or Cash for Clunkers (C.A.R.S.) Deals, by About Guide to Cars Aaron Gold.
