Stefani Germanotta (born March 28, 1986), better known by her stage name Lady GaGa, is a singer-songwriter dance-pop, electronica musician signed to Interscope Records. On April 8, 2008, she released her first single "Just Dance".

As a child Germanotta attended the Convent of the Sacred Heart School. Having learned piano by ear at the age of four, Germanotta went on to write her first piano ballad at thirteen and began performing at open mic nights by the age of fourteen. At the age of seventeen she was one of twenty people in the world to have gained early admission to the Tisch School of the Arts at NYU, where she studied music. By age twenty she began writing songs for Interscope Records artists, such as the Pussycat Dolls. Lady GaGa performed at the Miss Universe 2008 pageant in Vietnam, singing her debut single, "Just Dance".
