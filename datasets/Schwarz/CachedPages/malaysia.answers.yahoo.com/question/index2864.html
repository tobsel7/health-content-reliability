    In my view no. The reason that people invest in gold is to provide themselves with a hedge against a falling dollar and inflation. Gold has no real use, and more can be mined up from the ground as it is abundant and reusable, so the price of it really just depends on speculation about inflation and a falling dollar.

    Gold prices have reached unprecedented highs in recent years and I think that this is unjustified. In order for these prices to be sustainable, inflation would really have to hit hard. Unfortunately even then, the price of gold already reflects this possibility so it likely wouldn't rise much higher.

    If you are looking to invest in gold consider gold mining stocks. These companies make a profit off of mining gold and selling it. This way you have the opportunity to make money even if gold stays at the same price.

    However it is difficult to find any reasonably priced gold stocks at this point in time. Most gold stock prices have outpaced the rate of recent gold growth, so even if gold continued to rise in price these stocks would be overvalued.

    The only thing that seems close to reasonably priced is Freeport MacMoran (FCX). However I would look to better alternatives besides gold.

    Oil offers a good alternative because unlike gold, oil has a real use. It powers our global economy and without it we are in serious trouble. Furthermore, we are actually running out of it and many geologists agree that in a few hundred years we won't be able to find it anymore. Afterall there were only so many dinosaurs who died and gave us our supply of oil.

    Additionally oil prices rise for many of the same reasons as gold. When inflation occurs, oil prices rise. Falling dollar, higher oil price.

    Best of all, oil is not up on speculation like gold is right now. It's actually lower than it was a few years ago. Some oil stocks appear to be highly undervalued (so that is where i would invest long term).

    Good examples might include BP, ECA, or RIG. These oil plays offer dividends and great long term growth potential.

    Consider talking with a stock broker or financial advisor before investing.
