Is this a good time to invest in bonds?

Sept, 1996 | Comments

  • facebook
  • twitter
  • digg
  • buzz

Whether or not we should invest in bonds depends on 2 considerations. The first is whether the interest rate is high enough. The second consideration, which is even more important, is whether interest rates are headed higher.

Currently the interest is at low levels. The return on bonds is lower than what you can expect to get from many other investments. As Kerene Harvey points out (see page 5) the U.S. and Canadian economies are headed in different directions and Canadian inflation and interest rates continue to be low. From that point of view bonds cannot be considered a great investment.

The second consideration is the direction of the interest rates. As we all know, when the interest rates increase bond prices fall and when the interest rate falls bond prices increase. Given this simple relationship, which way are the interest rates headed? This is a difficult question to answer. While it appears that Canada's economy will put no great pressure on interest rates, inflation may rise in the U.S., pushing up interest rates. If this happens, then Canada may have to raise its interest rates to be competitive with U.S. rates. This can be bad news for bond-holders.

Overall, this may not be the best time to invest in bonds. Try and look for higher yielding alternatives (for an example, see page 4). If you must invest in bonds use the barbell strategy (buying bonds with different maturity years) to minimize risk.
