Floyd Landis, OUCH part company, embattled cyclist's career over at age 34?

Floyd Landis is again without a team, and it's likely the embattled cyclist, who last week during the Amgen Tour of California confessed to doping and implicated several former U.S. Postal Service teammates, is done with the sport.

Landis, 34, last year rode with OUCH, the squad sponsored by long-time supporter Dr. Brent Kay, the physician who resurfaced the cyclist's hip.

Landis finished 23rd overall in the 2009 Tour of California, but left the team following the season.

In 2010, Landis was set to sign with Rock Racing, the now amateur team owned and run by outspoken businessman Michael Ball. But Ball couldn't get a pro license for his squad.

Instead, Landis signed with the Bahati Foundation, the new team that subsequently added OUCH again as part of its official name.

With Bahati, Landis had his best result since 2006 when he finished second in April at the Tour of the Battenkill, a one-day race upstate New York.

Following stage 4 of the Tour of California, Landis' doping revelations became public and created massive media interest. As a result, the Bahati Foundation team announced May 28 it has "parted ways with the cyclist."

Correspondingly, OUCH Medical Center has withdrew its sponsorship of the squad.

“Due to the media intensive environment, the team and Floyd Landis have decided to amicably part ways,” read the team's statement. “We support his courage to come forward in an effort to facilitate a drug free and fair sport."

“The team is working to update sponsorship and continue supporting the mission of the Bahati Foundation. The Foundation supports youth in inner-city through the sport of cycling and GIVEBACK programs.”

Since he turned professional in 1999, Landis has ridden for: Mercury Cycling Team, US Postal Service, Phonak Hearing Systems, OUCH Bahati Foundation.
