Adam Lambert

  • Date of Birth: January 29, 1982
  • Birth Location: Indianapolis, Indiana

Woman Magnetism 64

"Keep speculating" was Adam Lambert's coy response to questions about his sexuality during the American Idol competition. That said, there was nothing shy about the lip-locking photos of him and another man that were publicly leaked. While a dedicated segment about the topic on The O'Reilly Factor offered no new insights (except to remind us that Bill O'Reilly is still a loser), a Rolling Stone cover promises to settle bets on Adam Lambert once and for all.

If the whispers are true, that still doesn't mean that he has no appeal to women. It's the opposite. Katy Perry has been spotted wearing Adam Lambert jackets, while VH1 contributor Michelle Collins confessed to the singer that she wants to have his children. Adam Lambert's painted fingernails and flamboyant wardrobe (tight pants, platform shoes) also make him a female favorite. Not only can he melt their hearts with song, but they can go shopping together -- in all of the same departments.

Success 52

Adam Lambert hit all the right notes on American Idol to become a feverish fan favorite and arguably the most popular second-place finisher in the show's history. Initially labeled (somewhat negatively) by Simon Cowell as "theatrical," Adam Lambert has used his acting background to enhance his stage presence and create a larger-than-life persona to match his substantial range. He's as comfortable singing U2 as he is Tears for Fears, and he has the hit digital singles to prove it.

While some American Idol alumni have earned a place in the lonely land of obscurity (how's it going, Ruben Studdard?), Adam Lambert's indisputable talent and knack for the theatrical (much like his idol David Bowie) are enough to make him stand out alongside fellow Idol success stories Carrie Underwood and Taylor Hicks. Now a flamboyant fixture on the musical list of "next big things," Adam Lambert just has to figure out which musical styles to settle on and who to sing them with.

Adam Lambert Why Is He Famous?

American singer Adam Lambert is a singing genius who should have won American Idol, at least according to his fans -- and even the winner, Kris Allen. The modern musical hybrid of David Bowie and Freddie Mercury, Adam Lambert is also one of the few performers on stage who can still look cool while wearing shoulder cages, fingerless gloves and handcuffs. Or maybe you just think he looks lame. Either way, you can't help but have an opinion.

Adam Lambert Biography

Adam Lambert was born in Indiana, but his family's relocation to California a year later makes the Golden State ultimately responsible for cursing him with painted nails, hot pants and the latest in women's eye makeup. As a youngster in San Diego, Lambert learned the virtues of music from his DJ dad, Eber. Not only a fan of the sounds, Lambert would also come to idolize the stylings of Queen, David Bowie and Michael Jackson, with some Aerosmith and Led Zeppelin thrown in for good measure.

Musical performance and acting went hand in hand for Adam Lambert as he was growing up. At 10, he made his stage debut as the blanket-clutching Linus in a production of You're a Good Man, Charlie Brown. After beginning vocal training with artistic vets Alex Urban and Lynne Broyles, Adam Lambert performed around town in local productions of Grease, Chess and Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat. The one-two punch of singing and acting instilled a sense of wonder and dazzle that would become impossible for Adam Lambert to shake.

adam lambert on american idol

After graduating from Mt. Carmel High School, Adam Lambert moved to Orange County for college, but dropped out only a few weeks later. An artist first and a student later, he settled in Los Angeles instead, where he re-appeared onstage in 2004's The Ten Commandments, starring Val Kilmer, and sang at venues like the Upright Cabaret. He also immersed himself in the glam scene as a performer in The Zodiac Show, an underground variety from the mind of The Pussycat Dolls' Carmit Bachar.

While working as an understudy for a production of Wicked, Adam Lambert was artistically unhappy. Something was missing. On the encouragement of his fellow cast members, he auditioned for American Idol and made the show with a blistering performance of "Bohemian Rhapsody." Over the course of the Idol season, he would score legions of fans and make glam-punk fashions cool again thanks to winning performances of Michael Jackson's "Black or White," U2's "One" and Led Zeppelin's "Whole Lotta Love." He also won over the notorious Simon Cowell, who initially called him "theatrical" and dismissed his cover of "Ring of Fire" as "indulgent rubbish," but later gave him a standing ovation for his version of Tears for Fears' "Mad World."

adam lambert post-idol

As American Idol drew to a close, Adam Lambert was expected by many to emerge victorious, but he suffered a surprise loss to his friend and Idol roommate, Kris Allen. While there were murmurs that an AT&T promotion in Allen's home state of Arkansas helped him win the competition, Lambert rose above the debate and was grateful that the overall experience had landed him a music career. Kris Allen responded by saying that Lambert had been the show's best singer. Go figure.

On the heels of his Idol finish and several successful Billboard 100 digital singles ("Mad World" peaked at No. 19 and "A Change Is Gonna Come" hit  No. 32), music fans are eager to hear where Adam Lambert will turn up next. At present, he's working on his debut album, a project that promises to be a hefty mix of musical styles. In addition, he is pursuing a possible collaboration with Queen -- a fitting move for the Freddie Mercury-inspired singer.
