Nadya Suleman (Octomom)

  Nadya Denise Doud-Suleman Gutierrez (born Natalie Denise Suleman, July 11, 1975) known as Nadya Suleman in the media and 'Octomom' in pop culture, is an American woman residing in La Habra, CA, who came to international attention when she gave birth to octuplets in January 2009. She already had six children. The circumstances of the multiple birth led to controversy in the field of assisted reproductive technology as well as an investigation by the Medical Board of California of the fertility specialist involved.

Curiosity of the octuplets' delivery turned to public anger when it was learned that the single, unemployed mother had been caring for her six other children with the help of food stamps and Social Security disability payments for three of the youngsters. Suleman said some of the disability money was spent on in vitro fertilizations, which was used for all 14 of her children. 1 

Early Life

Suleman was born in Fullerton, California to parents Angela and Ed Doud Suleman who married in 1974 in Las Vegas. The couple divorced in 1999. Angela Suleman is now a retired school teacher and Ed Doud has traveled back to Iraq, where he was born, to earn money as a translator in order to assist his daughter’s financial burdens. 2 

Marriage

Nadya Suleman and Marcos Gutierrez, a producer manager, married in 1996 but separated in 2000 due to difficulties in conceiving children together. Guitierrez filed for divorce in 2006, and the couple’s divorce was finalized in 2008. 3 

Education

Suleman graduated high school from La Puente High School and received her Bachelor’s degree in child development from Cal State Univeristy, Fullerton. Suleman went back to school in 2009 to complete a Masters of Science degree in counseling at Cal State University, Fullerton, in hopes of being able to earn more money for her 14 children. 4 

Children

David Beaudoin, Suleman’s long time friend she knew before her husband, was the supposed sperm donor of all 14 children. Suleman had tried to get pregnant with artificial insemination and fertility drugs before resorting to in-vitro fertilization. Suleman had given birth to six children a few years before her octuplets. 5 

Octuplets

At 10:48 a.m. on Jan. 26, 2009, Suleman delivered her six boys and two girls at Kaiser Permanente Bellflower Medical Center after only 23 weeks of pregnancy. The babies were thus delivered nine weeks premature. Doctors, who originally expected seven newborns, delivered her octuplets through Caesarian section, and the delivery lasted five minutes. Dr. Alejandro Vazquez, who was in his fourth year of residency at the time, found and delivered the eighth baby. Forty-six members of the hospital staff and four delivery rooms were used during the delivery process. The octuplets weighed between one pound, eight ounces and three pounds, four ounces. Two of the newborns were placed on ventilators and one infant needed oxygen. Seven of the infants stayed in the hospital for two months before they were discharged from the hospital. The eighth infant, Jonah, who was born with a small cleft on his lip, had to stay at the hospital until April 13 when he gained enough weight to be released to Suleman’s La Habra residence. 6 

Suleman gave her octuplets the Biblical names Noah, Jonah, Jeremiah, Josiah, Isaiah, Maliyah, Makai, and Nariyah. All eight have the middle name “Angel” and the last name “Solomon.” 7 

Suleman estimates that she spent at least $24,000 on her in-vitro fertilizations, which cost $6,000 each and says she paid for the procedures by working 16-hour days as a psychiatric technician. A portion of the $30,000 inheritance she received after her aunt died contributed to the expenses of conceiving her twins in. Suleman was able to freeze the leftover eggs, which were used to conceive the octuplets. 8 

Living arragements

When her children left the hospital in March 2009, Suleman had already bought a $565,000 four bedroom three bathroom, 2,583 square- foot house in La Habra to replace the three-bedroom La Habra house of Suleman’s parents which was near foreclosure due to $20,000 worth of unpaid mortgage payments. Suleman claims that photographs of her children along with media interviews and donations financed the new house. However, Suleman’s father is alleged to pay a $110,000 down payment on the house. 10 

Publicity

Interviewed on shows such as NBC ‘s Dateline with anchor, Ann Curry in February 2009 and Dr. Phil in March 2009, Suleman has inevitably been an object of public attention since the birth of her octuplets.

“Octomom: The Musical,” a musical created by Chris Voltaire, began on June 19 in Los Angeles. Voltaire said he never met Suleman or anyone associated with Suleman but that he didn’t need to because everything regarding the mother of 14 is so public.

Physical appearance

Suleman has also come under speculation after a noticeable weight loss only a year after her octuplet delivery. Sporting a bikini in Star magazine with no trace of loose skin or stretch marks, Suleman claimed she lost 150 pounds, transforming her 270 pounds to 120 pounds without plastic surgery. 14 

Suleman was also speculated to have gone under the knife to more closely resemble Angelina Jolie, a celebrity she deeply admired and sought to emulate. 15 

Threats

In April 2009, someone was suspected of throwing a baby car seat through the back window of Suleman’s gray Toyota Sienna. 12

Suleman has also dealt with multiple death threats and acts of vandalism to her La Habra residence along with protests against her decision to raise 14 children with such financial difficulties. 13 

Custody challenged

On July 27, 2009, Suleman appeared in court at the Lamoreux Justice Center in Orange, California to defend her rights as the mother of her children. Paul Petersen, a child-rights activists and former child star along with Gloria Allred his attorney, sued Suleman on allegations that the octomom was exploiting her children through photographs and media for financial compensation. Peterson and Allred asked for a guardian to protect the business endeavors of Suleman’s children. 16 

Orange County Superior Court Judge Gerald Johnston appointed lawyer Norbert Bunt as the guardian who would investigate and report on the children’s finances. On January 9, 2010, ordered that Bunt cease to investigate Suleman’s finances any further because no evidence was presented that showed that Suleman was guilty of any financial misconduct. 17 

Psychological state

Several of Suleman's hired nurses and publicists have also completely cut their ties with Suleman, citing psychological inconsistencies. 18 

Fertility

In May 2009, Suleman checked into the hospital for the removal of benign fibroids growing in her uterus. The procedure will prevent Suleman from having more children. 9 

Doctor, hospital staff disciplined

Michael Kamrava, who facilitated both of Suleman’s in-vitro fertilizations for her six children and her octuplets was as a member of The American Society for Reproductive Medicine until the medical group banned him from his practice. Kamrava of West Coast IVF in Beverly Hills, planted six embryos into Suleman’s uterus despite the ASRM’s one to two embryo recommendation for a woman of Suleman’s age. Kamrava also facilitated the fertilization of a 49-year-old woman who delivered quadruplets without health insurance. 19 

In total, 27 employees were terminated from Kaiser Permanente Bellflower Medical Center for not respecting patient privacy laws in regards to Suleman’s medical records. 20
