Michael Jackson
The King of Pop
(August 29, 1958 – June 25, 2009)


Michael Joseph Jackson was an American musician, entertainer, and widely regarded pop icon, whose successful music career and controversial personal life have been at the forefront of pop culture for the last quarter-century.


Jackson began his musical career at the age of seven as the lead singer of The Jackson 5 and released his first solo recording, Got to Be There in 1971, while remaining a member of the group. He began a full-fledged solo career in 1979 and formally parted with his siblings in 1984.
*See related wiki: The Jackson 5

In his solo career, Jackson recorded and co-produced the best-selling album of all time, Thriller, which was named as the world's best-selling album at the 2006 World Music Awards. It has worldwide sales exceeding that of 104 million. Michael Jackson has received thirteen Grammy awards and charted thirteen number-one singles in the United States.



Throughout his four-decade career, Michael Jackson has been awarded numerous honors including the World Music Award's Best-Selling Pop Male Artist of the Millennium, American Music Award's Artist of the Century Award and the Bambi Award's Pop Artist of the Millennium Award.

The King of Pop had been one of the few to have been inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame twice (once as a member of The Jackson 5 in 1997, and as a solo artist in 2001). He is also an inductee of the Songwriters Hall of Fame. Raymone Bain, Jackson's PR, claims that Jackson has sold over 750 million units worldwide.

Michael Jackson is also one of the very few artists who holds multiple titles in Guinness World Records.

From 1988 to 2005, Jackson lived on his Neverland Ranch property, on which he built an amusement park and private zoo for economically disadvantaged and terminally ill children.

His frequently held sleepover parties received disparaging media coverage after it was revealed that children frequently shared his bed or bedroom. These first came to light when he was accused of child sexual abuse in 1993. His sleepover parties were brought into the spotlight again in 2003 during the TV documentary Living with Michael Jackson. This resulted in Jackson being tried, and later acquitted, of more child molestation allegations and several other charges in 2005.

Michael Jackson has 3 kids: Paris, Prince Michael and Prince Michael Jackson II. The court had not found Michael Jackson to be guilty of the child molestation cases but his public image had been tarnished ever since.



Michael Jackson's last major concert tour was back in 2001, after he released the Album, Invincible. He was about to make a come back with a series of 50 concerts that would have been held in London around July 13, 2009. All tickets have already been sold out in just a few minutes after the announcement had been made for This Is It concert .

On June 25, 2009, Michael Jackson was was rushed to a Los Angeles-area hospital by paramedics who found him not breathing when they arrived at his home. Later that afternoon Jackson was pronounced dead at approximately 2:25 p.m. local time (21:25 UTC). , the cause of death is believed to be a cardiac arrest.

His sudden demise had taken the world by a wave of surprise and shock. Various celebrity notables, politicians and his fans had spoken out about his tremendous loss in the entertainment and music industry.

CNN had released a TV Special about the Legendary King of Pop entitled: Michael Jackson, The Man on The Mirror on June 27 and 28, 2009.

His memorial service was held at the Staples Center in Los Angeles on July 7, 2009. It had been publicly announced that there wouldn't be any procession after the service, and this was to reduce the number of people lining on the roads and the freeways. When Michael Jackson's motorcade proceeded from the Forest Lawn Mortuary to the Staples Center, the Los Angeles Police Department had blocked all traffic to ensure that his funeral would go smoothly.

At the memorial, Michael Jackson's casket was placed before the stage and the audience floor. It was broadcasted live around the world by ABC, CNN, Fox, MSNBC, NBC and E!, and had also been shown all around the web via live streaming. Several wireless carriers had also made the footage available through their mobile TV services.



Michael Jackson is survived by three children: Michael Jr., Paris, and Prince. He was not currently married, having previously divorced from Lisa Marie Presley and then Deborah Jeanne Rowe.

Michael Jackson in the World Guinness Book of Records:
Most Successful Concert Series - Guinness Book Of World Records
Michael Jackson sold out for seven nights at Wembley Stadium, London, England in the summer of 1988. A total of 504,000 people saw Michael perform July 14-16, 22-23, and August 26-27, 1988.

Biggest Selling Album Of All Time - Guinness Book Of World Records
Michael Jackson's "Thriller" Album is the biggest selling album of all time, with over 50 million copies sold worldwide. Thriller is also the biggest selling U.S album with sales of 25 million copies.

80's Most #1 Hits
By The End of the 1980's Michael Jackson had more #1 hits than any other artist for the decade.

Michael Awards
Michael has more awards than any other artist.

Entertainer Of The Decade
With the #1 (Thriller) and #2 (Bad) ranked albums in the world Michael was the 1980's Entertainer Of The Decade.


Most Grammy Awards - Guinness Book Of World Records
Michael won a record breaking 8 Grammy Awards in 1984, more than any other artist in one year.

Largest Contracts - Guiness Book Of World Records
$890 million (Sony Music) Contract, with prospective earnings of $1 billion.

Greatest Audience - Guiness Book Of World Records
The highest-ever viewership was 133.4 million viewers watching the NBC transmission of Super Bowl XXVII on June 31, 1993. Michael was spotlighted during the half-time performance.

Highest-Paid Commercial Spokesperson - Guiness Book Of World Records
Pepsi Cola paid Michael Jackson $12 million to do 4 TV commercials.



Bad Tour - Guinness Book Of World Records

Michael Jackson's world tour brought in a record gross revenue of over $124 million during September 1987-December 1988.

100 Million Records

Michael has sold over 100 million singles and albums outside of the U.S.

Michael Jackson is the first person in the 37 year history of the chart to enter at # 1, with his single "You Are Not Alone". Michael broke his previous record held by his single "Earth Song" which debuted at #5.
