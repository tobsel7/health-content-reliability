Infighting escalates among Obama birthplace doubters

By MARTIN WISCKOL
COLUMNIST
THE ORANGE COUNTY REGISTER
mwisckol@ocregister.com
Story Highlights

Man who claims he has Obama's Kenyan birth certificate criticizes 'birther' attorney Orly Taitz.

Next Article »

The sideshow spectacle of Laguna Niguel attorney Orly Taitz trumpeting the supposed illegitimacy of Barack Obama's presidency now features a key ally calling her a liar and giving a scalding critique of her lawyering abilities.

The purpose of this column item is to unveil that criticism.

Lawyer Orly Taitz is a leader in the effort to prove that Barack Obama was born in Kenya. Photo by Paul Bersebach, The Orange County Register

A few days ago, I reported that two of Taitz's potential witnesses filed affidavits saying she'd asked them to lie in federal court (click here for that story). One of those men, Larry Sinclair, claims to have been Obama's lover. The other, Lucas Smith, claims to have bribed a Kenyan military official and obtained Obama's Kenyan birth certificate.

Obama's "certificate of live birth" and birth announcements in two Hawaiian newspapers – all viewable online – combine with investigations by Hawaii's chief health official and the Annenberg Public Policy Institute to provide seemingly irrefutable proof of Obama's natural-born citizenship.

But an energized minority continues to gather at online outposts – fueled by Taitz's occasional appearance in the mainstream media – to perpetuate the suspicion that Obama's presidency is illegitimate because he was born in Kenya. And while federal courts have repeatedly thrown out Taitz's lawsuits without letting them go to trial, infighting among the "birthers" keeps the drama lively.

"It began to become clearer and clearer to me that Orly Taitz had no understanding of the law," Smith wrote in his affidavit, which also included details of what he alleged was Taitz's extramarital sex life.

Specifically, Smith points to Taitz's lack of effort to prove that her documents are authentic and prove that Obama was born abroad. The legal burden of proof is, in fact, on Taitz.

"She still claims that the burden is on Obama, the defendant, to prove the certificate is not his," Smith wrote. "Outside of paying attention to her own voice at length, Orly Taitz has the attention span of a small child."

Taitz categorically and specifically denied Smith's allegations. However, she apparently still believes Smith when it comes to the Kenyan birth certificate.

POLL DANCING

GOP gubernatorial candidate Meg Whitman went on a radio ad blitz in October, but that only accounts for a part of a huge variance in polls on the race. At the beginning of the month, the Field Poll showed Whitman at 22 percent and fellow Republican Tom Campbell at 20 percent. At the end of the month, a Capitol Weekly/Probolsky poll showed Whitman at 34 percent and Campbell at 12.5 percent.

Part of that difference, according to pollsters at both outlets, was that the Field Poll polled all Republican voters and right-leaning independent voters, while the Probolsky poll focused on the voters in that pool with a high likelihood of actually casting ballots.

A few days after the Probolsky poll, an L.A. Times/USC poll showed Whitman at 35 percent and Campbell at 27 percent. The difference from the Probolsky poll? Probolsky listed Campbell as a "law professor" while the Times listed Campbell as a "former congressman." Previous elected posts aren't allowed as ballot designations.

However, the Times poll found that two-thirds of the voters had "no impression" of Whitman or Campbell – and that all polling matching the Republicans against Democrats show Jerry Brown with a broad lead. Republican Steve Poizner was running well behind the others in all three polls.
