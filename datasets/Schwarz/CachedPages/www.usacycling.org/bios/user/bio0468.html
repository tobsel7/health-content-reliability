Introduction
Floyd Landis suffered a broken hip in a pre-training camp crash which kept him off the bike early in 2003. He made a rebound however and played a key role in Lance Armstrong’s fifth Tour de France victory and in the team’s Vuelta A Espana victory. He concluded the season with a small group of riders just seconds behind the winner at the World Championships. The former Junior National Mountain Bike Champion switched to road cycling in 1998 and had his break-out season in 2002 when he made a podium finish at the Dauphine Libere.
Personal
Floyd graduated from Conestoga Valley High School in Lancaster, PA in 1994. He moved to California to help with his mountain bike training.
Favorite Pastimes
Floyd spends time with his wife, Amber, and daughter, Ryan.
How They Got Here
Floyd used to ride mountain bikes in the woods with a high school friend. It was simply their mode of transportation to go fishing, but eventually, they stopped fishing and just rode. Floyd jumped right into the big events, and within one year was the U.S. Junior National Champion in Cross Country. In 1997, he was U23 National Champion, and by 1998, was riding at the top of the world’s biggest professional mountain bike races. In 1998, Floyd made the switch to road and was convinced he could be a pro when he placed third in the ’99 Tour de l’Avenir. USPS saw him as an up and coming climber, and made him the offer of a lifetime.
Did You Know?
• Landis is known for pulling wheelies.
• He is a climbing and time trial specialist.
• NORBA named him pro rookie of the year in 1995 for mountain biking.
