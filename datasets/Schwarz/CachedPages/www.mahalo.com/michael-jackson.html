  • Michael Jackson was one of the most popular performers of all time and the highest grossing musician of the 1980s. His career began at the age of eight when he joined The Jackson 5 along with four of his brothers. His precocious singing and dancing skills helped the group produce a string of number one singles, including "ABC" and "I Want You Back," and established him as a bona fide child star.


    Jackson died on June 25, 2009, after suffering cardiac arrest in his Los Angeles, California, home.Los Angeles Times: Michael Jackson is Dead (June 25, 2009) An autopsy was performed on Jackson on June 26, 2009. On August 24th, it was revealed that Jackson died as a result of "lethal levels" of the anesthetic propofol.LA Times: 'Lethal levels' of anesthetic propofol killed Michael Jackson (August 24, 2009) Jackson's body was entombed in a mausoleum at the Glendale Forest Lawn Memorial Park in California on September 3, 2009.Winnipeg Sun: Michael Jackson laid to rest (September 4, 2009)

    Jackson, who had not performed a full concert since 2001, was scheduled for a 50-date residency at London's O2 Arena beginning in July 2009.   • Julien's Auction House Lawsuit

    TMZ.com reported on March 4, 2009, that Jackson's production company filed suit against Julien's Auction House claiming that they had no right to auction off several items belonging to him. Such items included rhinestone gloves and socks, the gates from Neverland and the hands from Edward Scissorhands.TMZ: Jacko Suing Over Creepo Auction (March 4, 2009) Julien's Auction House responded to the suit by saying that Jackson signed contracts giving them permission to auction off the items. The auction was scheduled to take place in April.TMZ: Jacko Approved the Event (March 5, 2009)

    The Sun reported on February 12, 2009, that Jackson had a skin infection, possibly MRSA or a staph infection, and was being treated with antibiotics. According to the report, it was a possibility that he may have contracted the infection from a plastic surgery procedure on his nose.The Sun: Did Jacko get skin bug from nose job? (February 12, 2009)

  • Illness

    The Sun newspaper reported on December 22, 2008, that Jackson was suffering from a genetic disease, A1AD (a1-Antitrypsin deficiency), that left him 95% blind, barely able to speak and in need of a lung transplant.The Sun: Jacko Needs Lung Swap (December 22, 2008)


    News of the disease was reportedly leaked by Jackson's biographer, Ian Halperin. Halperin also revealed that Jackson was suffering from emphysema and chronic gastrointestinal bleeding.The Sun: Jacko Needs Lung Swap (December 22, 2008)


    Jackson's brother, Jermaine Jackson, was said to have confirmed the report stating, "He's not doing so well right now. This isn't a good time."The Sun: Jacko Needs Lung Swap (December 22, 2008)


    Dr. Tohme, the official spokesperson for Michael Jackson, called the rumors a "total fabrication."People: No Truth to Michael Jackson Lung-Transplant Story (December 23, 2008)


    A1AD is a genetic disease that can afflict children and adults. The disease can lead to emphysema and cirrhosis.MERCK: α1-Antitrypsin deficiency

  • Career

    In 1979, Jackson released the Quincy Jones-produced solo album Off the Wall, which included "Rock With You" and "Don't Stop 'Til You Get Enough" and sold millions of copies. Despite the smash hit status the album achieved, it still couldn't compare to the massive success of its follow up, 1982's Thriller. Thanks to a track list that included "Beat It," the title track (not to mention its accompanying video in which Jackson transforms into a zombie), and "Billie Jean." Thriller sold a staggering 27 million copies in the United States alone, spawned seven top 10 hits and became the best selling album of all time.Yahoo! Finance: Michael Jackson's Thriller - The World's Best-Selling Album of All Time (November 7, 2008)

    Jackson's success continued with hit albums like Bad and Dangerous, but his reputation and public image soon shifted from his music to allegations of child molestation. Although he was never found guilty, the charges, as well as his reclusive behavior, caused his reputation to flounder.
