Floyd Landis

Categories: Cycling | Sports | News | People In The News

    • Born: October 14, 1975
    • Birthplace: Lancaster County, Pennsylvania
    • Former teammate of Lance Armstrong
    • Number of 2006 victories: 6
    • Banned for two years and forced to forfeit Tour de France title for doping
    • June 30, 2008: Landis lost final appeal of doping decisionSI.com: Landis Loses Doping Appeal Before Sport's Highest Court (June 30, 2008)
    • Ordered to pay $100,000 in legal fees to U.S. Anti-Doping AgencySI.com: Landis Loses Doping Appeal Before Sport's Highest Court (June 30, 2008)
    • Landis' ban from cycling lasts through January 2009The New York Times: Landis Loses Final Doping Appeal (June 30, 2008)

  • Floyd Landis is a professional cyclist who won the 2006 Tour de France. In September 2007, Landis was stripped of his championship title for allegedly using performance-enhancing drugs. Landis appealed the decision but lost when a panel ruled on June 30, 2008, that his original positive doping test was valid.



    On September 26, 2008, it was reported that Landis planned to appeal to U.S. Federal Courts that his positive steroid doping test which stripped Landis of his Tour de France title was not fairly heard by sports arbitrators.ESPN.com: [Landis claims arbitrators had conflicts of interest]


    On February 15, 2010, it was reported that Landis was wanted by the French government on charges of hacking into the anti-doping agency's computer.http://www.cnn.com/2010/SPORT/02/16/cycling.landis.hack.warrant/index.html?hpt=T2
