    The Car Allowance Rebate System (CARS), better known as Cash for Clunkers, was a U.S. government-sponsored economic stimulus plan designed to boost auto sales by giving consumers up to $4,500 off the purchase of a new fuel-efficient vehicle when they traded in a less fuel-efficient vehicle. The Cash for Clunkers program went into effect on July 1, 2009, and came to end on August 24, 2009. The Cash for Clunkers program resulted in 690,114 clunker trade-ins and finished slightly under budget at $2.88 billion.http://blogs.thecarconnection.com/marty-blog/1034657_cash-for-clunkers-ends-under-budget-sort-of

  • Cash for Clunkers Eligibility Criteria

    1. Trade-in vehicle must be less than 25 years old.http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_Allowance_Rebate_System
    2. Must purchase new vehicle, or lease for a minimum of 5 years.http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_Allowance_Rebate_System
    3. Trade-in vehicles must get 18 or fewer MPG (some exceptions for trucks and vans apply).http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_Allowance_Rebate_System
    4. Trade-in vehicles must be registered and insured continuously for the full year preceding the trade-in.http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_Allowance_Rebate_System
    5. Trade-in vehicles must be in drivable condition.http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_Allowance_Rebate_System
    6. The program runs from July 1, 2009 through November 1, 2009 (or while funds last).http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_Allowance_Rebate_System
    7. The trade-in vehicle will be scrapped, and the scrap value will be added to the CARS rebate.http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_Allowance_Rebate_System
    8. Customers must pay sales tax on the rebate.http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Car_Allowance_Rebate_System

  • What Happens to the Clunkers?

    1. Car buyers bring their eligible clunkers to the car dealership and get a credit of $3,500 to $4,500 on a new fuel-efficient car.
    2. The car dealers then have to submit a reimbursement claim to the government.
    3. On the lot, the dealers are supposed to replace the engine's oil with a solution of sodium silicate in order to irreparably damage the engine.
    4. The clunkers are then sent to the scrap yard, and the recyclers have 180 days to sell any parts before the clunker must be shredded.

    (August 17, 2009 edition of Time Magazine, page 14, by Justin Fox)
