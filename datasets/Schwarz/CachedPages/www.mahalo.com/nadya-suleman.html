    People Magazine reported on May 4, 2009, that attorney Gloria Allred has filed a petition with the court requesting that a guardian be appointed to ensure the rights of Suleman's infant octuplets since she plans to feature them and her other children in a reality show.   • Octo-Mom vs Angels in Waiting

    • On March 23, 2009, Radaronline reported Suleman has decided to discontinue the services of Angels In Waiting, the non-profit charity organization that had offered, for a fee, to provide continuous care for the octuplets as well as for the six other children. Angels in Waiting had been caring for the children since March 17, 2009, as the first of the octuplets were released from the hospital. Suleman has said she will hire her own nannies instead. Members of Angels in Waiting will be taping an episode of Dr. Phil on March 24, 2009. Suleman hopes to make another appearance on Dr. Phil to rebut any negative statements the nursing organization may make.Radar: Oct Mom Fires Angeles in Waiting (March 23, 2009)

    On March 24, 2009, Kaiser Permanente Bellflower Medical Center spokesperson Jim Anderson confirmed the hospital will stay on track to release her remaining four newborn babies despite Suleman's public falling out with the Angels in Waiting nurses. Anderson said, "That is between AIW and Nadya Suleman. The AIW is a whole separate thing. Obviously, they want to make sure she has enough people to take care of them, but, in terms of who the caregivers are, that is pretty much her decision."US Magazine: Octuplets To Be Released Despite Mother Firing Nurses (March 24, 2009)

  • Octuplets

    Nadya Suleman, gave birth to octuplets at the Kaiser Permanente Bellflower Medical Center in California, on January 26, 2009. The babies, six girls and two boys, were born healthy despite being nine weeks premature.   • Back Injury

    In 1999, Suleman was injured while trying to restrain a patient at the mental hospital where she worked as a psychiatric technician. According to reports, Suleman was hit in the back with a desk by a patient. In August 2008, it was found that 90% of her back injury was work related and 10% was due to pregnancy. In December 2001, Suleman said she had spent most of her days in bed and was unable to care for her newborn. Suleman, who is now unable to work because of her injury, collected over $165,000 in disability payments from the state of California.SFGate: Birth of octuplets worsened mom's back injury (February 6, 2009)

  • NBC Interview

    On February 6, 2009, NBC aired part of an interview by Ann Curry with Suleman. The full interview will air on the Today show on February 9, 2009, and on Dateline on February 10, 2009. Suleman said that she dreamed of having a "huge family" because she lacked a "feeling of self and identity" and defended herself against the accusations that she is "selfish."MSNBC: Octuplet mom defends her 'unconventional' choices (February 6, 2009) On the same day, it was revealed that Suleman, who suffers from a "depressive disorder," collected nearly $168,000 for a work-related back injury she sustained in 1999.SFGate: Birth of octuplets worsened mom's back injury (February 6, 2009)

  • Suleman's Doctor?

    In February 2009, Suleman said the treatment for the in vitro fertilization that resulted in the octuplets was provided by a physician at the West Coast IVF Clinic in Beverly Hills, formerly known as the West Coast Infertility Medical Clinic. The treatment is believed to have been provided by the clinic's medical director Michael Kamrava.San Francisco Chronicle: Octuplets mom identifies her fertility clinic (February 9, 2009)

  • Death Threats

    Suleman's publicist, Joann Killeen, reported in mid February 2009, that Suleman had been receiving death threats and decided to move into a secure location for her family's safety. She has since left that location as the threats are no longer considered high risk.US Weekly: Octuplet Mother Received Death Threats (February 12, 2009)

  • Angelina Jolie Syndrome?

    Members of the press have noticed an uncanny resemblance between Angelina Jolie and Suleman, many of them asking whether Suleman was intentionally attempting to emulate Jolie, including suggestions of plastic surgery. While Suleman has denied any sort of extreme fandom of Jolie, a source revealed that Suleman had attempted to contact Jolie through mail on multiple occasions. Jolie was allegedly "creeped" out by Suleman.Los Angeles Times: Angelina Jolie 'creeped out' by octomom Nadya Suleman

  • Vow of Celibacy

    In an interview with RadarOnline published June 5, 2009, Suleman made what was described as a "bombshell revelation" about her sex life. She said that the octuplets' biological father—whom she had previously claimed was only a friend and sperm donor—was actually the last person she had had sex with, and that their sexual relationship had ended over ten years before. According to Suleman, she had since taken a "vow of celibacy."RadarOnline: Octomom: Donor Daddy Was Last Last Man I had Sex With
