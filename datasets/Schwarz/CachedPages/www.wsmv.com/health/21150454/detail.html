H1N1 Vaccine Can Have Side Effects

CDC Staff Says Side Effects Similar To Regular Flu Vaccine

Reported By Marc Stewart

POSTED: 1:49 pm CDT September 29, 2009
UPDATED: 6:13 pm CDT September 29, 2009

NASHVILLE, Tenn -- As with any medication or vaccine, there is always concern about side effects. The H1N1 vaccine is no exception.

Special Section: H1N1 Flu Outbreak | Watch This Story

Even the seasonal flu vaccine has side effects.

Doctors at Vanderbilt have studied 220 people as part of the national H1N1 clinical trials. They said side effects from the shot are possible, but it was small portion of those tested.

The symptoms can "begin soon after the shot and usually last 1 to 2 days," said Centers for Disease Control and Prevention staff.

Vanderbilt's William Shaffner, a nationally recognized expert in infectious diseases, said the side effects from the H1N1 vaccine are the same as the typical flu vaccine. These side effects include a fever of one or two degrees above normal and redness or soreness near the vaccine injection.

"They've heard more about the testing of this vaccine then they do with the normal influenza vaccine, so that raises questions in their mind because they're not familiar with the procedure," said Schaffner.
