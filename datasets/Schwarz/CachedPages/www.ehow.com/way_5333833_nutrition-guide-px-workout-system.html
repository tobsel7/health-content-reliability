Nutrition Guide for the P90X Workout System

P90X is a high-intensity workout program that will help to burn fat and build huge amounts of muscle in 90 days. The backbone of any diet and exercise plan is the nutritional plan that goes with it. If the nutrition plan is faulty, the exercise plan will not deliver the results you desire. You have to stick to the nutrition plan to gain the best results. The P90X Nutrition Plan is divided into three phases: the Fat Shredder; the Energy Booster; and the Endurance Maximizer.

    Phase 1: The Fat Shredder

  1. The key to this phase is protein, and a lot of it. Eating foods that are high in protein and low in fat will help burn off unwanted fat and build muscle mass simultaneously. Protein contains the raw materials needed to rebuild catabolized muscle tissue (muscle tissue that was damaged during a workout). This rebuilding process is what builds strength, endurance and size. Do not rely on one or two sources of protein throughout this phase. There are plenty of sources that will provide the protein necessary to maximize your results during this phase. The best resources are lean meats, including chicken breast, turkey breast, lean cuts of beef and tuna. Other sources include protein powders (for making post-workout protein shakes), beans and lentils. Switch up your protein sources regularly for variety.

    The recommended protein/carbohydrate/fat ratio during this phase is 50/30/20.

    2. Phase 2: The Energy Booster

  2. During this phase you will decrease the amount of protein consumed and increase the amount of carbohydrates consumed while keeping your fat calorie intake the same as in Phase 1. This calorie mixture will provide the energy you need to perform better and to continue to see results. High-quality complex carbohydrates include vegetables, whole grains, beans, lentils and fruits. A salad with a lean meat tossed in (like chicken or steak) is a great meal during this phase. Choose breads that are made of 100 percent whole wheat or whole grains for a nice turkey sandwich. A few eggs, whole wheat toast and fruit make a great breakfast. Use olive oil while cooking and include a fatty fish, like salmon, for a few meals to get the fats that are actually good for your body.

    The recommended protein/carbohydrate/fat ratio during this phase is 40/40/20.

    3. Phase 3: The Endurance Maximizer

  3. Complete the P90X 90-day program with this mix of calories that is now heavier on the carbohydrates and lighter on the protein. The fat percentage again remains the same. Increasing the amount of carbohydrates and decreasing the amount of protein you consume will supply your body with the nutrients it needs to maximize the level endurance you will gain during the final phase of the P90X program. Focus on whole grains, fruits and vegetables during this phase and you will be well on your way to finishing what you started.

    The recommended protein/carbohydrate/fat ratio during this phase is 20/60/20.
