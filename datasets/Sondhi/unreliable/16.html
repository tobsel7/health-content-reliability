Headaches And Natural Cures

Headaches can be irritating or they can be debilitating. While some people experience headaches every once in a while and take medication to get over them, others have such severe headaches that they need to be tested for other conditions and often end up missing work. In most cases, the cause of headaches is never known.

  

Doctors are hesitant about prescribing medication to treat headaches unless they are so severe that they actually impair someone from living a full and active life. In most cases, doctors will advise either taking over the counter pain relievers or using a natural method. Headaches are one of the few ailments that the medical community will actually encourage you to seek out alternative medicine. This is because the medications that are on the market today to treat headaches have dangerous side effects.

If you suffer from headaches and have been told by your doctor to use natural treatments, here are some that you might try which will work:

Ice packs

Ice packs have been used for years to treat headaches and they still work. You can usually find ice packs at your drugstore. There are some that come with chemicals and simply open up and go on your head and those that need to be filled with ice and put on your head. Try using an icepack to relieve headaches and you will see that they do work.

Acupuncture

The idea of sticking pins and needles into parts of your body may seem horrific, but acupuncture has been used for centuries to cure pain caused by headaches. Acupuncture must be performed by a certified acupuncturist in order to be effective but it does work well to relieve the pain from headaches.

Chiropractic Massage

Many chiropractors believe that the reason people get headaches is that their spine is out of whack. By straightening out the spine, a person can have their headaches relieved. Many people swear by the methods used in chiropractic care for just about everything. Those who practice this type of alternative medicine claim that it can not only cure back and leg problems, but headaches as well.

Aromatherapy

Aromatherapy is an ancient healing art that has been used to treat headaches for ages. It works well whether you are using massage oils or inhaling the different herbal oils. Using aromatherapy to treat headaches is nothing new as this method of healing has been around for centuries.

Other ways to treat headaches naturally and without drugs include taking a nap. In some cases, taking a nap will alleviate the headache. You will wake up feeling rested. Even if you cannot nap, you may find that headaches are relieved by closing your eyes. In many cases, headaches are the result of eyestrain, especially for those who work on the computer all day long or do a lot of reading.
