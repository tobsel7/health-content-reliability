Get an accurate diagnosis - this is important!

Getting the right treatment for stomach ulcers and h. pylori starts with getting the right diagnosis. Correct diagnosis is the key to whether the treatment for your stomach ulcers is going to work or not.

Make sure that your doctor is aware of H. pylori (there are still doctors out there who have the "no acid - no ulcer" mind set) - if you have an ulcer there is an 80% - 90% chance you will be infected with H. pylori.

If you have an H. pylori infection, eradication of the bacteria is vital. If your ulcer is caused only by prolonged use of NSAID's then you will receive a different treatment. For stomach ulcers that have been left unattended, complications such as bleeding, and in some cases, stomach or gastric cancer can occur.

There are several methods of diagnosis used to determine whether a patient is infected with H. pylori. The different methods can be simple or complicated, inexpensive or costly. The main methods of diagnosis are endoscopy (invasive but accurate), while other non-invasive tests can also be used, such as X-rays, Breath test, or a stool antigen test.


When do I need to seek medical advice?

As soon as you start showing any symptoms of an ulcer, you need to see a doctor for a diagnosis for H. pylori. Once your doctor has diagnosed you properly - he will prescribe a treatment to eradicate H. pylori based on his diagnosis - and usually it will be based on his knowledge of drug therapies.

Why do Drug Therapies fail?

There are 3 main reasons for drug treatment failure.

  • Firstly, H. pylori is resistant to most single doses of antibiotics and latest tests show increased resistance to dual and triple dosages as well.

  • The third reason is that many patients are unable to cope with the side effects of the various prescribed drug therapies, and have to abandon the treatment before the full course has been completed.
