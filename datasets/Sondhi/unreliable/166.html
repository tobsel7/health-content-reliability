Home Remedies for Asthma

Introduction

Asthma is a disease related to respiratory system. When the lungs get filled with cough or any foreign body enters into the windpipe it causes partial blocking or irritation. This condition is termed as asthma. This is a common disease which affects nearly 10-20% of the total world population. It affects people of all the ages; however it is very common amongst the children. In most of the cases the disease being mild is often not recognized and is left untreated.

In asthma, airways get narrowed. These narrowed airways may lead to coughing, wheezing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath. These symptoms get worst at night time. It is observed that it affects twice as many boys as girls in childhood; more girls than boys develop asthma as teenagers, and in adulthood, the ratio gets equalized say 1:1 males to females.

The conditions of seriousness vary from person to person. In some cases as mild as no effects and in extreme cases also death. It is generally common in cold air, dusty environment, dust and acid fumes humid conditions, eating allergic food product, emotional stress, tobacco smoke, or even respiratory viral infection.

In Canada itself about 3 million people are affected, and approximately 20 children and 500 adults die each year from asthma. However, most deaths from asthma can be prevented by adequate treatments. There is a general trend of increased deaths and hospitalizations from asthma recorded in all the industrialized countries of the world, including Canada.

Prevention from Asthma Attack

The following are some of the risk factors which asthma patients must avoid:-

(i) Avoid using perfumes.

(ii) During your asthma attack always sit up straight or bend forward. Never lie down.

(iii) Avoid food which can increase the chance of asthma attack Common foodstuffs that increase the chance of this are milk products, animal foods, or food that takes more than two hours should be avoided.

(iv) Take special care that your bedroom is free from allergen material.

(v) Dust your room often but not with broomstick instead if possible use a vacuum cleaner.

(vi) Wear a scarf round your mouth and nose during cold weather to be on the safer side. This will warm the air and prevent the cold air from entering in and reaching the sensitive airways

(vii) Use a plastic cover over your mattress and if possible wash this in hot water every week.

(viii) If you have an air conditioner than see to it that you change its filter periodically.
