Our hair is consists of a type of protein called keratin. A single hair consists of a hair gleam, a root that exists underneath the skin, and a follicle, from which the hair root grows. At the lower end of the follicle is the hair bulb, where the hair's color pigment, or melanin, is formed.

Most people lose about 50 to 100 head hairs a day. These hairs are replaced soon after they are lost. They nurture back in the similar follicle on your head. This quantity of hair loss is absolutely normal so people who are experiencing such a hair loss need not to worry at all. Although not essential but if you're losing more than that then you should definitely go for a medical treatment. The therapeutic expression for hair loss which involves losing enough hair that a person has evidently thin or hairless patches is known as alopecia.

If you have extraordinary hair loss and not even sure about the cause of it then the best idea is to see a medical therapist. A therapist can conclude why the hair is falling out and put forward a treatment that will correct the underlying problem, if necessary.

What causes hair loss?

  • According to a detailed research a number of rationales can cause hair loss like:
  • You may suddenly loose a large amount of hair after 3 to 4 month’s serious illness.
  • Hormonal problems may also cause hair loss.
  • If your thyroid gland is overactive or under active, your hair may fall out
  • Many women notice hair loss about 3 months after they've had a baby. This loss is also related to hormones.
  • Some medicines can cause hair loss. This type of hair loss improves when you stop taking the medicine.
  • Certain infections can cause hair loss.
  • Fungal infections of the scalp can cause hair loss in children. The infection is easily treated with antifungal medicines.
  • Hair loss may occur as part of an underlying disease, such as lupus or diabetes. Since hair loss may be an early sign of a disease, it is important to find the cause so that it can be treated.

 

There are a number of factors that causes hair loss and baldness. Either the diet you take lacks certain vitamins or nutrients that are required for the active hair growth or some serious illness can also lead to excessive hair loss and in severe cases cause baldness.
