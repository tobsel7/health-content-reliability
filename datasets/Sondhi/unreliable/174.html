Home Remedies for Alzheimer's Disease

Alzheimer's disease is the most common form of dementia and a condition which gradually, destroys brain cells and causes a decline in mental functions. Alzheimer's disease can be classified as a progressive brain disease that gradually destroys the person’s memory and ability of learning, reasoning, making judgments, communicating and carrying out daily activities. Alzheimer's disease occurs mostly in the elderly group of people, who have crossed the age of 65 years.

As the disease progresses, it changes the person's behavior and personality and also create delusions and hallucinations. The areas of the brain which have control over memory and thinking skills are first affected. As the disease advances, the cells in the brain region die. This is the primary step in causing irreparable damage to the brain.

There are several indicated causes for Alzheimer's disease, but science has not yet been able to pick out one definite cause. Since the last fifteen years, medical discoveries have revealed several factors that can play an influencing role in causing Alzheimer's disease. Family history of the disease is also strongly influential, and thus Alzheimer's disease can be labeled as a hereditary disease.

The mortality rate of people in this disease is extremely high. It is seen that most people suffering from the Alzheimer's disease die within four to six years of the affliction, while in some people the affliction may also last till twenty years. But it always results in death of the person suffering from the disease. The reason of death could be old age related complications, which are very common since Alzheimer's disease strikes only when the old age sets in.

Prevention from Alzheimer's Disease

There are several lifestyle-related factors that can reduce the chances of Alzheimer's disease in people. The following are some points that must be noted:-

(i) Mental exercises must be urged on children since a very young age to nourish their brain. This becomes a habit in later life, when as adults, they try to use their brain to its fullest extent. This makes the brain stronger and helps to keep all mental problems like Alzheimer's disease and dementia away.

(ii) It has been found, after stringent tests, that leading an active lifestyle really helps in keeping Alzheimer's disease and other mental problems away. A daily exercise routine is must, and also there should be serious attempts at losing weight. The role of exercise in treating Alzheimer's disease is particularly true in women.

(iii) There are several dietary considerations to be taken into account. The following are some important points:-

a) All vitamins are very essential for the proper functioning of the brain. Vitamin A, vitamin C and vitamin E are chiefly important. The last two vitamins are needed as antioxidants which maintain the proper functioning of the brain. But the role of the vitamins of the B complex can also not be ignored. Folates are especially important.

b) Proteins are necessary for their reserve of amino acids. A high concentration of amino acids in the blood helps to keep Alzheimer's disease away.

(iii) There seems to be a deep-seated relationship between the proper functioning of the blood and the intelligence quotient of the brain. Direct relations have been unearthed revealing that people with high blood pressure have much more chances of developing dementia and Alzheimer's disease in their later lives. Hence, treating blood pressure conditions at the earliest could also be a preventive therapy for Alzheimer's disease.

One misconception floating around very prominently about Alzheimer's disease is that taking supplements of estrogen could be a cure. This is certainly not the case, and produces no more than a placebo effect. The estrogen does work in the initial stages, but the brain goes back to its impaired state in a few months' time.
